Who is Jacqueline Bracamontes?

Jacqueline Bracamontes, is a well-known Mexican actress, who is known for her roles in Alegrijes and Rebujos (2003-2004), Sortilegio (2009), Killer Women (2010), The Beloved (2017), Loli’s Luck (2021) and others.

Apart from being an actress, she is also a former Mexican queen, who is the winner of the 2000 Nuestra Belleza México national pageant.

Jacqueline Bracamontes- Age, Bio, Parents, Siblings, Ethnicity, Education

Actress Jacqueline was born on December 23, 1979, in Guadalajara, Jalisco, Mexico. As of 2023, her age is 43.

Likewise, she holds Mexican nationality and is of Caucasian ethnicity. As per the astrological chart, her birth sign is Capricorn.

Moving on to her parents, she was born to her father, Jesús Bracamontes, and mother, Jacqueline Van Hoorde. Her father is the former coach, who used to serve in the Mexican football team, Chivas Rayadas Gel Guadalajara. However, her father currently works as a football analyst for the Hispanic-American TV network Univision.

In addition to siblings, she has a sister named, Alina and a brother named, Jesús Bracamontes Jr. She is the eldest child of her parents.

There is no info on her early educational life at the moment. However, as a model, she studied Communication science at the Instituto Tecnológico de Estudios Superiores de Occidente.

Jacqueline Bracamontes- Professional Life, Career

Being passionate about acting since a young age, Jacqueline started her career with Acafest, Premios TVyNovelas, and Fiesta Mexicana. Then, she hosted various shows and programs.

She was then called off by the former universe Lupita Jones to take part in the Nuestra Belleza México after seeing her on a magazine cover.

In August 2008, she grabbed a chance to host a Mexican sports radio show on Vision AM. Afterward, she grabbed the chance to host the 2002 FIFA World Cup.

In 2018, she got an honorable chance to be part of the Telemundo network’s coverage of the 2018 FIFA World Cup. She was the host of La Voz’s first and second season host.

Acting Career

Apart from being a host, Bracamontes is also a versatile actress. In 2003, she appeared in Rosy Ocampo’s Mexican telenovela, Alegrijes y Rebujos.

After a three-year gap, in 2006, she landed her first lead role, in the Mexican novel Heridas de Amor.

Following that, she was cast as Lucia, a nun in the 2007 feature film, Las Cosas Suceden.

In the year 2008, Jacqueline starred in the comedic Mexican telenovela Las Tontas No Van al Cielo.

On the other hand, as a voice actress, she voiced the character of Susan Murphy, in the Spanish-language version of Monsters vs. Aliens. It was released in 2019.
