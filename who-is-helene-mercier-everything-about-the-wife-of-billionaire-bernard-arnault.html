Who is Hélène Mercier? Know about her background

Hélène Mercier is a globally renowned Canadian concert pianist who is quite popular for her extraordinary piano skills and the brilliant work she did throughout her musical career. Helene is best known for being the wife of billionaire Bernard Arnault, all across the world.

Hélène was born on February 5, 1960, in Montreal, Canada, and started taking piano lessons at the very young age of six. She quickly created a career out of her passion. Due to her dedication to learning, she went on to win first-place prizes in national-level competitions all across the country. Mercier pursued honors at the Quebec and Canadian Music Competitions and then became a laureate of Prague’s International Chamber Music Competition. She studied music in Vienna, Austria, by the age of 15. She later studied at the Juilliard School in New York and continued working for her passion for music by performing at festivals across the world.

Mercier’s career highlights

Hélène Mercier has earned respect and fame all across the world, and all this has helped her successfully accomplish a lot of things throughout her career. As a duo with Louis Lortie, Mercier has released several recordings with Chandos containing works by popular names such as Ravel, Poulenc, Mozart, Rachmaninoff, Saint-Sans, and Vaughan Williams. All these recordings got great reviews from all across the world. She also got the chance to record with globally renowned names like Edward Gardner’s directorial BBC Philharmonic, the Bergen Philharmonic Orchestra conducted by NeemeJärvi, and Sir Andrew Davis.
